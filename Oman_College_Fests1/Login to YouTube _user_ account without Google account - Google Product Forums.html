In November 2017, my agency was able to log into its agency channel at https://www.youtube.com/user/DCAGeorgia and upload new content.

We are trying to access it today and getting "account not found". Our username has always been some...@dca.ga.gov, but it doesn't like this address. Between Marketing and IT we use this account on a regular basis from multiple computers so I am confident the username(address)/password combination is correct.

Reading other help posts about "claiming" a brand account, results in some...@dca.ga.gov account not found too.

Is there a backend way to login and then associate it to a Google account. We are cool doing whatever, we just need access to the channel and content and since we use the channel to conduct business the URL needs to stay the same.

Any help is greatly appreciated!

The Lady from UNCLE said:

It is not possible to sign into YouTube with anything BUT a Google Account, and the YouTube product www.youtube.com/user/DCAGeorgia is already associated with its owner Google Account (and, therefore, doesn't need to be re-associated with a Google Account).  That is the first thing.

Now to the difficult part-  because Google Accounts are personal affairs and carry the first and last name, as well as the date of birth of the person who clicked "I agree" (note the singular, not "we") when signing up to Google, it is not possible to assist where the single-user Google Account is clearly used by multiple users.  You will need to ask each and everyone of them what they did to make the Google Account not exist since 20th December 2017 when it was uploaded to.  Someone will have swapped out the email address, possibly even deleted the user if it was a G Suite user or managed mail.  

Tip-  look for the message I sent to the YouTube's owner.  Hopefully it is picked up by the current email address and there isn't a correspondence email giving a false positive.
